01 / Monitoring

Persipica Pulse

Pulse runs scheduled prompt tests across ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini, tracks whether your brand is recommended in buyer prompts, verifies citations, and gives your team a shared record of what changed and when.

Flexible workspaces

Per-workspace configuration of brand, category, competitors, and alert channels. Suitable for a single brand or a multi-client agency setup.

  • Single-brand or multi-client workspaces
  • Competitor displacement flags
  • Slack and email alerting

Cross-model scoring

Asynchronous audit jobs run against all three major models across buyer journey stages, with a deterministic scoring pipeline so results are comparable over time.

  • ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini
  • Stage-level prompt clusters
  • Score movement over time

Deterministic scoring pipeline

Versioned prompt catalog, provider adapters, shared web grounding for citation prompts, and centralised scoring so every run is directly comparable.

  • Versioned prompt catalog
  • Provider adapters per model
  • Shared web grounding for citations

Evidence ledger

Every run stores the exact prompt, the full model output, and citation verification results so your team can trace any score shift back to its source.

  • Prompt and output history
  • Verified citation ledger
  • Review-ready audit trail

03 / Implementation

Implementation support

For teams that want Persipica involved after the diagnosis. This covers content restructuring, authority signal building, structural fixes, and a post-implementation re-audit to confirm progress.

Strategy and roadmap

A prioritised roadmap built from audit findings. Every recommendation ties back to a specific citation gap and buyer stage.

  • Content gap prioritisation
  • Authority signal mapping
  • Structural change recommendations

Execution and re-audit

Content restructuring, source coverage work, and entity definition. All output is documented so your team can maintain it independently. Full GEO Programme clients receive a post-implementation re-audit.

  • Content and page structure improvements
  • Source and citation coverage work
  • Follow-up measurement against the baseline
