# GEO Glossary

Key terms used in Generative Engine Optimization.

## A

**AI Visibility**: The degree to which a company, product, or concept appears in AI-generated responses across platforms like ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity.

**AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)**: An older term focused on getting content into Google's featured snippets and voice search answers. GEO is broader, targeting generative AI platforms specifically.

**Authority Signal**: Any third-party indicator that AI models use to judge a company's credibility, reviews, press coverage, analyst mentions, Wikipedia presence, and community discussions.

## B

**BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front)**: A content structuring principle where the first paragraph under any heading provides a standalone, factual summary that an AI model can extract and cite directly.

**Brand Query**: A prompt that asks AI about a specific company by name. Most companies score well on brand queries.

**Buying Intent Query**: A prompt where the user expresses readiness to purchase or evaluate vendors. These are the highest-value queries for pipeline generation.

## C

**Citation Rate**: The percentage of queries in which a company is mentioned in AI-generated responses. Calculated as (queries cited ÷ total queries) × 100.

**Citation Rate by Stage**: Citation rate broken down by buyer journey stage (Discovery, Comparison, Brand, Buying Intent).

## D

**Discovery Query**: A prompt where a buyer describes their problem without naming any specific company. This is where new pipeline originates and where most companies score 0%.

## E

**Entity Clarity**: How clearly and accurately AI models understand what a company does, who it serves, and how it differs from competitors.

**Entity Confusion**: When AI models misidentify a company's category, capabilities, or competitive positioning, common for companies with broad product portfolios or recent pivots.

## G

**GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)**: The discipline of ensuring a company is cited and recommended by AI systems when users ask questions relevant to that company's category.

**GEO Audit**: A structured assessment of AI visibility across buyer journey stages, platforms, and competitor benchmarks.

## L

**LLM (Large Language Model)**: The AI systems that power ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and similar platforms. These models generate responses by synthesizing learned knowledge with (optionally) real-time web retrieval.

**llms.txt**: A plain-text file placed at a website's root that provides structured information about a company specifically for AI crawler consumption.

## R

**RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)**: A technique where AI models combine their training knowledge with real-time web retrieval to generate responses. Perplexity uses RAG by default; ChatGPT and Claude use it optionally.

## S

**Semantic Density**: The concentration of factual, specific, citable information in a piece of content. AI models prefer content with high semantic density over vague marketing language.

**Share of Voice (AI)**: The proportion of AI-generated responses in a category that mention a specific company relative to competitors.

## T

**Training Corpus**: The dataset of web content, books, research, and structured knowledge that an AI model learned from during training. Companies well-represented in training corpora have structural advantages in citation.

**Third-Party Corroboration**: Independent verification of a company's claims across sources like G2 reviews, press coverage, analyst reports, and Wikipedia. Claude in particular requires dense third-party corroboration before citing a company.
